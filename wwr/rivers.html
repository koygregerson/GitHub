The Clark Fork River

Location: The Clark Fork River winds its way through the scenic landscapes of Montana and Idaho. It begins in the Silver Bow Mountains of Montana and flows for over 300 miles before joining the Pend Oreille River in Idaho. Scenic Beauty: One of the most appealing aspects of the Clark Fork River is its stunning natural beauty. As it meanders through the Rocky Mountains and various canyons, rafters are treated to breathtaking vistas of rugged terrain and lush forests. The river offers a mix of gentle stretches and exciting rapids, making it suitable for both beginners and experienced rafters. Rafting Opportunities: The Clark Fork River provides a range of rafting experiences, from leisurely family floats to adrenaline-pumping white-water adventures. There are various sections of the river, each offering a different level of difficulty, so adventurers of all skill levels can enjoy their time on the water. Wildlife Viewing: While rafting down the Clark Fork, you'll have the opportunity to witness an array of wildlife, including bald eagles, ospreys, deer, and occasionally even moose and bears. The river and its surrounding areas are a haven for nature enthusiasts. Historical Significance: The Clark Fork River has historical importance as it played a significant role in the region's development during the late 19th century, serving as a vital transportation route during the Gold Rush era.
