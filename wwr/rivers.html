Rivers

The Clark Fork River

Location: The Clark Fork River winds its way through the scenic landscapes of Montana and Idaho. It begins in the Silver Bow Mountains of Montana and flows for over 300 miles before joining the Pend Oreille River in Idaho. Scenic Beauty: One of the most appealing aspects of the Clark Fork River is its stunning natural beauty. As it meanders through the Rocky Mountains and various canyons, rafters are treated to breathtaking vistas of rugged terrain and lush forests. The river offers a mix of gentle stretches and exciting rapids, making it suitable for both beginners and experienced rafters. Rafting Opportunities: The Clark Fork River provides a range of rafting experiences, from leisurely family floats to adrenaline-pumping white-water adventures. There are various sections of the river, each offering a different level of difficulty, so adventurers of all skill levels can enjoy their time on the water.

The Snake River

Location: The Snake River is a majestic waterway that flows through the western United States, traversing states such as Wyoming, Idaho, Oregon, and Washington. It is renowned for its diverse landscapes and captivating scenery. Scenic Beauty: Rafting on the Snake River offers a captivating journey through some of the most breathtaking scenery in the country. The river cuts through deep canyons, winds across high plateaus, and provides stunning views of the Teton Range in Wyoming. Rafting Opportunities: The Snake River caters to a wide range of rafting experiences. Depending on the section you choose, you can encounter anything from calm, scenic floats to thrilling rapids suitable for more experienced rafters. Popular sections include the Snake River Canyon in Wyoming and Hells Canyon, the deepest river gorge in North America.
